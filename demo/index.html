How to use iikshana

Get your courtroom running with real-time translation in four simple steps. No technical expertise required.

1
Connect your audio source
Plug in a USB microphone, lapel mic, or route system audio directly into iikshana. The platform auto-detects available input devices and recommends the optimal configuration for your courtroom layout.
2
Select your languages
Choose the source language or enable auto-detect — iikshana will identify the spoken language within the first few words. Then select one or more target languages for simultaneous translation output.
3
Start live translation
Click the "Start" button and speak naturally. iikshana processes audio in real time, producing translated text and optionally audio output within under 2 seconds. A live scrolling transcript is displayed throughout.
4
Share & export
When the session ends, download the timestamped transcript as a PDF or plain text file. Share the ASL stream link with hearing-impaired participants, or save the recording for official court records.

About iikshana

We built iikshana because language should never determine whether someone can participate in their own legal proceedings.

"Our mission is to ensure every person in a courtroom — regardless of language or hearing ability — has full, equal, and dignified access to justice. iikshana means 'perception' or 'vision' in Sanskrit. We believe that clarity of understanding is a fundamental right."
